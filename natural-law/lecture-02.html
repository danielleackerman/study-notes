  • Method: arrive at knowledge by excluding known negatives — deductive reasoning.
    • vs. cataphatic inquiry = reasoning in the affirmative = inductive reasoning.
  • Applied to rights and wrongs: define all wrongdoings → rights = every action that falls outside those parameters.
  • This is the logic stage of the Trivium applied to morality — the filtering, weeding-out process.

1903:12:10 — The Natural Law Transgressions (Wrongdoings)

Harmful actions capable of being taken against another being — the boundary conditions for breaking natural law.

1. Murder / Assault slide

  • Hebrew fifth commandment: lo tirtzach = do not MURDER (not kill — two different words).
    • Murder = taking life without right; initiation of violence.
    • Right to defensive force exists; right to murder does not, ever, under any circumstance.
    • Assault = bodily harm without right — never a right; analogous to attempted murder.

2. Rape slide

  • Coercing the free will of another person's sexual association — always wrong, no exceptions.

3. Theft slide

  • Taking property belonging to another that was obtained by them without harming others.

4. Trespass slide

  • Invading another's "lair" — the domain they use, own lawfully, and are responsible for — without permission; taking their security.

5. Coercion slide

  • Forcing someone through threat of violence to comply with your will against their own.
  • "If you don't do this I will harm you" — never a right; a wrongdoing in all circumstances.

Additionally: Lying

  • Theft of truth; withholding information needed for accurate informed decision-making.
  • "Thieving truth" — taking away the ability to make correct judgments based on available information.

2003:19:20 — All Wrongdoings Are Forms of Theft

  • COREQUOTE"Every harmful action a human being is capable of taking is a form of theft." slide
    • Open challenge, never defeated — no one has found a wrongdoing that isn't a form of theft.
    • Lying = theft of truth; murder = theft of life; rape = theft of free will; coercion = theft of free will; trespass = theft of security.
  • "Proper" ← right → property = that which you have a right to.
    • All rights are property rights.
    • All wrongs are theft of property.
    • Life is property; freedom is property; sexual autonomy is property; truth is property; rights themselves are property.
  • COREThe Crux of Natural Law:
    • A living being or their property must have been harmed for a natural law violation to have occurred.
    • Any action which does not cause such harm = a right.
    • QUOTE"No victim, no crime" — blanket statement; absolute.
    • Billions of people are in prison who have never harmed another living being.
    • Billions of people on Earth cannot define what a right is.
